Foldable Phones in 2025: Are They Finally Worth Buying?

Published March 18, 2025 • 5 min read

Foldable smartphones have been a fascinating experiment since their debut, but early models came with steep prices, fragile screens, and software that struggled to take advantage of the unique form factor. In 2025, the landscape looks dramatically different. Major manufacturers have iterated on hardware durability, app developers have optimized for flexible displays, and prices have dropped to levels that put foldables within reach of mainstream buyers for the first time.

Durability Has Caught Up

The biggest concern with early foldables was the crease in the display and the fragility of the folding mechanism. After several generations of refinement, manufacturers have largely solved these issues. Current foldable screens use ultra-thin glass layers that resist scratches and reduce the visible crease to a faint line barely noticeable during normal use. Hinge mechanisms have been tested to withstand hundreds of thousands of folds, far exceeding the typical lifespan of a smartphone. Water resistance ratings now match those of traditional flagship phones, removing another barrier that previously made buyers hesitant to invest in the form factor.

Software Finally Makes Sense

Hardware improvements mean little without software that takes advantage of the larger, flexible display. In 2025, both Android and third-party developers have embraced multi-window layouts, drag-and-drop interactions, and continuity features that let apps seamlessly transition between the folded cover screen and the unfolded main display. Productivity apps shine on foldables, allowing users to run a document editor alongside a video call or browse reference material while composing emails. Social media and entertainment apps have also adapted, offering immersive viewing experiences on the tablet-sized inner screen that simply cannot be matched on a standard smartphone.

Price and Value Proposition

Perhaps the most significant shift is affordability. Entry-level foldables from major brands now start at prices comparable to last year's traditional flagships. This pricing strategy, combined with trade-in programs and carrier deals, has made foldables accessible to a much broader audience. For users who want a large screen experience without carrying a separate tablet, foldables offer genuine value. The compromise between portability and screen real estate that defined smartphones for over a decade is finally being resolved by a device category that delivers both.

Foldable phones have matured from an expensive novelty into a practical choice. If you have been waiting on the sidelines, 2025 might be the year to make the switch.
